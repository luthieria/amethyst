# Planes[^https://youtu.be/p27uJvUhZrU?si=CTkcnH4kC9EkVfmM][^https://woodworkersinstitute.com/hand-planes-an-introduction]
## Types
### 1. Bench
#### 1.1. Smoothing (#3, #4)
- Use:
  - final smoothing.

![alt text](<Imagenes/Smoothing plane.png>)

##### #4
- The most common.
- Many furniture can be made just with it.
- → Great for beginners[^https://youtu.be/p27uJvUhZrU?si=CTkcnH4kC9EkVfmM][^Sellers].

#### 1.2. Jack, Fore (#5, #6)

- **Jack** ≈ **fore**.

- "Fore" ← "before" ← was used before other planes.

- Medium-length.

- Versatile.
- Use:
  - initial rough work;
  - flattening;
  - (sometimes) smoothing.

![alt text](<Imagenes/Jack plane.png>)

##### #5
- The most popular and important Stanley plane.
  - Perfect as the first / only owned plane.
- #5 width = #4 width.

###### Setup
1. **Aggressive** (traditional):
   - mouth: wide;
   - blade: cambered heavily.
2. **Fine** (works as a smoothing plane):
   - mouth: narrow;
   - blade: cambered very lightly.
   - use:
     - jointing tasks on smaller boards (a jointer replacement);
     - shoot edges;
     - level table tops.

##### #6
- Metal-bodied is hard to use. Prefer the wooden-bodied #6.
- Can be used as a jointer.

#### 1.3. Jointer (#7, #8)
- Use:
  - jointing edges,
  - flattening large surfaces.

![alt text](<imagenes/Jointer plane.png>)

- **#7** = "**triplane**".
- **#8** = "**jointer plane**".
- Little difference between #7 and #8.

##### Stanley Sweetheart Low-Angle
- Use: 
  - levelling and jointing small things;
  - shooting;
  - smoothing (although too big).

### 2. Block
- Blade: 
  - lower angle,
  - bevel up.
- No chipbreaker.

- Can be held with one hand.

- Use:
  - across the grain on the end grain;
  - general purpose: 
    - taking down a knot, 
    - smoothing small pieces, 
    - chamfering edges.

### 3. Speciality
#### 3.1. Rabbet
- For cutting rabbets (shoulders, grooves)

![alt text](<Imagenes/Rabbet plane.png>)

#### 3.2. Shoulder
- For trimming tenons and shoulders.

![alt text](<Imagenes/Shoulder plane.png>)

#### 3.3. Router
- For cleaning up grooves, dados, recesses.

![alt text](<Imagenes/Router plane.png>)

#### 3.4. Plough / Combination
- For cutting grooves and dados with adjustable fence and depth stop.

![alt text](<Imagenes/Plough plane.png>)

## Order of importance (= acquisition)

1. **#4**.
2. **#5**.
3. **Jointer** (wooden): #6 / #7 / #8.
4. (Optional) Stanley Sweetheart **low-angle**.
