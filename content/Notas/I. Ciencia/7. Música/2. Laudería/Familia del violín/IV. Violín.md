# II. NECK
## II.I. Neck Proper[^3]
- Pegs and their holes should be round (not oval) and fit together perfectly.[^1]

- In a humid climate, the wood at the body–fingerboard joint swells, leaning the neck toward the bridge. → For a humid climate, set the neck at a greater angle. Vice versa for a dry climate.[^2]

## II.II. Fingerboard[^3]
- Radius: 42 mm (along the entire fingerboard).
- Side thickness: 4.5 mm.

### Relief
- **Hollowness** = **relief**.

- Opinions:
	1. greatest in the mensur’s middle;[^4]
	2. distributed equally from end to end; measured halfway along the fingerboard.[^3]
- Ideally, less for steel strings than for gut strings.[^4]

- **Standard**:
	- **1 (E)**: ~ 0.75 mm / [^6]0.5 mm;
	- **4 (G)**: ~ 1 mm / [^6]0.7 mm.

- **Check**: move a ~ 10-cm straightedge along the fingerboard at the string positions, observing the gap against the light.

### Fitting on the Neck
- **Neck + fingerboard center thickness:**
	- **~ 50 mm from the root**: 20.5 mm;
	- **~ 25 mm from the nut**: 18.5 mm.
- The neck changes from a rounded shape at the root to oval at the nut.
